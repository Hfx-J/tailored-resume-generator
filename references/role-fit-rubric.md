# Role Fit Rubric

Use this rubric when selecting cards for a JD-specific resume.

## JD Parsing

Extract:

- Role target: title, seniority, team/function, domain.
- Priority 1: must-have requirements and hard filters.
- Priority 2: important but not absolute requirements.
- Bonus: preferred skills, industries, tools, or traits.
- Keywords: repeated nouns, tools, methodologies, and domain phrases.
- Language: use the JD language for final output unless the user asks otherwise.

## Card Scoring

Score each card out of 10:

- Requirement match: 0-3, direct overlap with Priority 1 and Priority 2 requirements.
- Evidence strength: 0-3, concrete deliverables, metrics, awards, users, or artifacts.
- Recency and depth: 0-2, recent and substantial work scores higher.
- Differentiation: 0-2, uncommon relevance, domain fit, or strong interview story.

Recommend the top 4-6 cards. For each recommended card, show:

- total score
- matched JD requirements
- strongest evidence
- missing facts or risks
- whether it is safe to use without user review

## Selection Rules

- Prefer verified cards over `needs_review` cards when scores are close.
- Prefer direct evidence over keyword similarity.
- Do not select a card if its `Do Not Claim` section conflicts with a JD requirement.
- If no card strongly matches a key requirement, mark it as a gap rather than forcing a weak match.
- For career-transition roles, include transferable cards only when the transfer logic is explicit.
